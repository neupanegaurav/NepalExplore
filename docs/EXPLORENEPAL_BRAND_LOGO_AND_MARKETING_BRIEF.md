# ExploreNepal Brand, Logo, and Marketing Brief

## 1. Purpose of This Document

This brief is written for:

- logo designers
- marketing teams
- brand strategists
- founders
- consumers who need a simple explanation of the brand

The goal is to make sure everyone understands what ExploreNepal stands for, what the logo should communicate, and how the brand should be presented to the public.

## 2. Brand Overview

### Brand Name

ExploreNepal

### What the Brand Means

ExploreNepal is about discovery, movement, culture, and connection. It is not only about finding a place on a map. It is about helping people experience Nepal with confidence and curiosity.

### Brand Promise

ExploreNepal helps people discover where to go, why it matters, and how to get there.

### Plain-Language Consumer Explanation

ExploreNepal is a location-driven discovery platform focused specifically on Nepal, combining destinations, local services, and cultural insights in one experience.

## 3. Brand Personality

The brand should feel:

- adventurous
- welcoming
- trustworthy
- modern
- locally grounded
- visually energetic

It should not feel:

- cold
- overly corporate
- too luxury-only
- too complicated
- generic like a standard map app

## 4. Core Brand Message

ExploreNepal connects travelers with the beauty, culture, and services of Nepal through one easy-to-use digital platform.

## 5. Logo Objective

The logo should instantly communicate three ideas:

- Nepal
- exploration
- movement or discovery

It should be simple enough to work as:

- an app icon
- a website header mark
- a social media profile image
- print material for tourism campaigns
- a small favicon or promotional badge

## 6. Recommended Logo Direction

### Best Overall Concept

A clean symbol that combines:

- a map pin or location marker
- a mountain silhouette
- a pathway, trail line, or upward movement shape

Why this works:

- the map pin signals location and discovery
- the mountain silhouette connects directly to Nepal
- the trail or upward line suggests travel, adventure, and progress

### Optional Supporting Motifs

These can be explored carefully, but should not make the logo too busy:

- sunrise behind the mountains
- prayer flag inspiration
- stupa-inspired geometry
- horizon line
- compass cue

## 7. Logo Style Guidance

### The Logo Should Be

- bold and recognizable
- clean at small sizes
- modern and mobile-friendly
- easy to remember
- suitable for both digital and print

### The Logo Should Avoid

- too many tiny details
- complicated illustrations
- clip-art style mountains
- too many colors in one mark
- symbols that look copied from generic travel brands

## 8. Color Direction

The current product already leans into bright, energetic colors. The logo and marketing system should stay aligned with that direction.

### Recommended Core Colors

- Primary blue: `#005CFF`
- Secondary cyan: `#00E5FF`
- Accent orange: `#FF6D00`
- Accent pink: `#FF007F`
- Accent teal: `#1DE9B6`

### Suggested Brand Use

- Primary blue should lead the identity because it feels confident, digital, and trustworthy
- Cyan can support freshness and movement
- Orange works well for calls to action and highlights
- Pink and teal should be used selectively so the brand stays strong, not noisy

### Simpler Logo Palette Recommendation

For the main logo, use:

- blue + white
- blue + orange
- black + white for flexible usage

This keeps the logo stronger and more practical than using all brand colors at once.

## 9. Typography Guidance

The typography should feel modern, readable, and friendly.

Recommended direction:

- clean sans-serif
- confident but not aggressive
- good readability on mobile screens

Suggested font styles:

- Inter
- Manrope
- Plus Jakarta Sans
- Sora

### Typography Personality

- headlines should feel bold and clear
- body copy should feel easy to read
- avoid highly decorative fonts
- avoid fonts that feel too formal or old-fashioned

## 10. Logo Deliverables the Design Team Should Create

The logo team should prepare:

- primary full logo
- icon-only logo
- horizontal logo lockup
- monochrome version
- light background version
- dark background version
- app icon version
- social avatar version
- favicon version

## 11. Logo Usage Rules

### Do

- keep enough spacing around the logo
- use approved brand colors
- ensure readability on small screens
- test on app icons and social media circles

### Do Not

- stretch the logo
- rotate the logo
- use too many gradients
- place the logo on visually noisy backgrounds without contrast
- add shadows and effects that reduce clarity

## 12. Consumer-Friendly Brand Story

ExploreNepal is built for people who want to discover Nepal more easily. It helps travelers find beautiful places, understand what makes them special, and connect with the local services they need along the way.

This is not just an app for searching places. It is an app for exploring Nepal with confidence.

## 13. Marketing Positioning

### Positioning Statement

For travelers and local explorers who want a smarter way to discover Nepal, ExploreNepal is a location-driven discovery platform focused specifically on Nepal, combining destinations, local services, and cultural insights in one experience.

### Key Marketing Pillars

- Discover more places
- Travel with more confidence
- Connect with local services
- Experience Nepal more deeply

## 14. Advantages to Highlight in Marketing

### For Travelers

- find places faster
- discover hidden gems
- learn before you go
- get directions quickly
- browse businesses relevant to your trip

### For Local Businesses

- get discovered by active travelers
- improve local visibility
- reach people at the moment they are planning where to go
- benefit from a tourism-focused platform

- tourists visiting Nepal
- young domestic travelers

### Secondary Audience

- hotels and restaurants
- guides and ticketing services

- local promotion partners

The marketing team can create campaigns around:

- hidden gems in Nepal
- weekend trips
- spiritual and heritage destinations
- scenic routes and viewpoints
- trusted local services for travelers
- "discover nearby" content

## 19. What the Consumer Should Feel When Seeing the Logo

When a person sees the ExploreNepal logo, they should feel:

- this app is about Nepal
- this app helps me explore
- this app is modern and easy to use
- this app can guide me somewhere meaningful

## 20. Final Recommendation for the Logo Team

If only one direction is chosen, the best option is:

"A modern map pin merged with a mountain silhouette, using strong blue as the primary color and a simple, clean wordmark."

This direction is the clearest, most scalable, and easiest for both the marketing team and consumers to understand.
