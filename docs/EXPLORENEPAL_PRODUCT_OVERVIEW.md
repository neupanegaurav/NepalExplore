# ExploreNepal Product Overview

## 1. What ExploreNepal Is

ExploreNepal is a tourism discovery app built to help people find, understand, and navigate places across Nepal. It combines map-based exploration, local business discovery, community spot submissions, and AI-assisted travel context in one mobile-friendly experience.

The app is designed to serve three groups at the same time:

- Travelers who want to discover places quickly and confidently
- Local businesses that want visibility among tourists
- Platform or tourism teams that want a simple way to review and manage new listings

## 2. Product Vision

ExploreNepal helps people move from "Where should I go?" to "I know where to go next."

Instead of forcing users to search across many websites, maps, blogs, and social channels, the app brings together:

- destination discovery
- nearby exploration
- local services
- route guidance
- cultural context

This makes the travel planning experience simpler, faster, and more engaging.

## 3. Who the App Is For

## 4. Core Features

### 4.1 Explore Map

The map is the heart of the app. Users can browse tourist spots visually across Nepal and understand where places are located before they commit to visiting.

Key capabilities:

- interactive map with destination markers
- category-based marker styling
- clustered markers for busy areas
- "Search this area" action for map-based discovery
- current location support
- quick access to spot detail pages

Why it matters:

- users can explore naturally, not just by typing keywords
- it feels more like travel and less like searching a database
- it helps users discover nearby opportunities they might otherwise miss

### 4.2 List View for Easier Browsing

Users can switch from map view to a list-based experience. The list groups places by category and shows distance from the user's current location.

Key capabilities:

- list view toggle
- grouped results by category
- distance-aware ordering
- image-led cards for fast scanning

Why it matters:

- some users prefer structured browsing over maps
- it supports quicker comparison across multiple destinations
- it improves accessibility for users who do not want to rely only on a map

### 4.3 Search and Category Filters

Users can narrow down destinations using keyword search and category chips.

Current category coverage includes:

- historical sites
- religious places
- nature trails
- viewpoints
- picnic areas
- sceneries
- mountains
- offroad riding
- cycling spots
- tourist agents
- hotels
- tickets
- guides
- dining

Why it matters:

- users can move from a broad idea to a specific plan quickly
- the app supports both inspiration and intent-driven search

### 4.4 Detailed Spot Pages

Every destination can open into a richer detail screen that explains what the place is and helps the user decide whether to visit.

Key capabilities:

- destination name and overview
- category label
- image gallery
- AI-generated travel and cultural insights
- location and route preview
- "Navigate Now" action

Why it matters:

- users get more confidence before traveling
- content feels more informative and premium
- it increases time spent in-app and strengthens trust

### 4.5 AI Insights

The app includes AI-generated destination insights to give users quick context about a place.

The insights focus on:

- cultural context
- historical anecdotes
- local lore
- practical travel tips

Why it matters:

- makes the app feel smarter and more memorable
- helps travelers connect emotionally with destinations
- gives ExploreNepal a stronger product identity than a basic map listing app

### 4.6 Featured Local Directory

The app includes a dedicated section for local businesses and travel services. This is where users can discover promoted or useful services relevant to travel.

Examples:

- hotels
- dining spots
- guides
- ticketing offices

Key capabilities:

- featured business listings
- promoted message highlights
- sort by featured status, price, or distance
- contact action
- directions action
- list and grid browsing

Why it matters:

- gives travelers useful service options in context
- opens a monetization path for the platform
- creates value for local businesses, not just tourists

### 4.7 User Spot Submission

Users can submit new places to the app through a simple form.

Key capabilities:

- destination name
- description
- category selection
- map pin location selection
- image upload flow
- submission status for review

Why it matters:

- allows the app to grow through community contribution
- helps uncover hidden gems beyond standard tourist lists
- creates a sense of participation and local ownership

### 4.8 Business Promotion Request Flow

Local businesses can request placement in the app through a promotion form.

Key capabilities:

- business details submission
- contact details
- location selection on map
- category choice
- price range
- promotion tier selection
- marketing message entry

Why it matters:

- supports partnership and revenue opportunities
- makes the platform useful for the tourism economy
- gives businesses a direct path into the app ecosystem

### 4.9 Admin Review Flow

The app includes a moderation layer for reviewing user-submitted locations.

Key capabilities:

- view pending submissions
- approve spots
- reject spots

Why it matters:

- improves quality control
- protects user trust
- keeps public content cleaner and more reliable

### 4.10 Theme and Experience Settings

The settings area provides basic app personalization and utility actions.

Key capabilities:

- system theme mode
- light mode
- dark mode
- sync action placeholder
- app info section

Why it matters:

- improves comfort for different user preferences
- makes the product feel complete and polished

## 6. What Makes the App Valuable

ExploreNepal is valuable because it combines four important functions in one product:

- discovery
- education
- navigation
- promotion

This is stronger than a basic map app because users are not only shown where a place is. They are also helped to understand why it matters, how to reach it, and what services are available around it.

## 7. Example User Journeys

### Traveler Journey

1. Open the app and browse the map
2. Filter by viewpoints or nature trails
3. Open a destination card
4. Read AI insights and view photos
5. Tap navigation and travel there

### Hidden Gem Submission Journey

1. User opens the Post tab
2. Adds a name, description, category, and map location
3. Uploads photos
4. Submits the place for review
5. Admin reviews and approves it

### Business Promotion Journey

1. Business owner opens the Local Directory
2. Chooses "Post Business"
3. Enters business and contact details
4. Chooses a location and promotion tier
5. Submits request for follow-up
